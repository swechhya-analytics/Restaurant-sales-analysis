# Restaurant Sales Analysis — Anonymous Japan-Based Restaurant (2022–2023)

## Project Objective
Small restaurants in Japan — and across the world — often rely on intuition rather than data when making decisions about inventory, staffing, and seasonal planning. This project challenges that by applying structured data analysis to two years of real sales data from a small Japan-based restaurant, with the goal of building an accessible, practical analytical framework that any small or medium-sized enterprise (SME) can understand and use.

## Key Questions
- How do sales fluctuate over time, and what drives those fluctuations?
- Are there identifiable seasonal patterns that could inform smarter planning?
- What insights can replace guesswork in inventory management and staffing decisions?

## Tools & Technologies Used
- Microsoft Excel – data cleaning, analysis, and visualization
- Google Sheets – collaborative data organization and reporting
- Real restaurant sales data (2022–2023) – primary data source

## Project Summary
This project analyzes two years of real sales data from a small Japan-based restaurant to uncover meaningful patterns in revenue, customer demand, and seasonal behavior. Findings are presented in a clear, visual format designed to be accessible to non-technical restaurant owners — demonstrating how simple analytical tools can replace intuition and support smarter business decisions around inventory, staffing, and seasonal planning.
